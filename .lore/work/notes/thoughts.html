Deck mechanics brainstorm

Initial notes on the two-deck structure: a mundane player deck versus a world deck of obstacles. The core tension is resolving world cards before they overwhelm you.

▦ Core structure

Player deck starts mundane. Different character archetypes produce different starter decks, each with a distinct feel from the first draw.

World deck is a set of obstacles. Each turn, you draw from both decks and spend player cards to satisfy and discard world cards.

▦ Turn structure

1Draw 4 player cards
2Draw 2 world cards
3Play player cards to satisfy world cards
4Discard resolved world cards

▦ Open questions

Numbers going up: Games in this genre rely on "numbers go up" and "numbers compound." That's a design risk if everything scales linearly. Worth deciding early whether satisfaction is binary (resolve or not) or accumulative (pile up points).
Point-based satisfaction: Search and Barricade might contribute typed points ("1 Hidden", "1 Block") rather than resolving obstacles outright. World cards would then require a threshold of the matching type. This creates partial progress and more granular decision-making.
