Player resource: Energy

DRAFT REQ-ENERGY

Energy is a per-turn player resource that gates a subset of cards. The player gains 1 energy at the start of every turn (including the first), unspent energy carries over with no cap, and certain cards require energy to play. This spec defines the resource, its lifecycle, and the cost gate. It does not prescribe implementation; that belongs to the plan.

Terminology guard: WorldCard already has a numeric cost field (the difficulty/clear-cost of a hazard, consumed by DealProgress and DestroyCardInHand.maxCost). Energy cost is a separate concept that lives on player cards. The two costs must not be conflated. Where ambiguity is possible, this spec says "energy cost."

State & data model

REQ-ENERGY-1 GameState carries an energy field Must

GameState gains an energy field: a non-negative integer representing the player's currently available energy. It is part of the serializable game state and threads through the pure reducer like every other field.

REQ-ENERGY-2 Player cards carry an energy cost (default 0) Must

Player cards have an energy cost: a non-negative integer. The cost is authored in card data (the catalog/world JSON, consistent with card-data-externalization), not hardcoded in engine logic. When a player card's data omits the cost, it defaults to 0 (free), so every existing card keeps its current behavior unless its data opts in.

The minted PlayerCard instance carries its energy cost so the engine can read it without re-consulting the catalog, matching how other per-card fields are minted.

REQ-ENERGY-3 Listen, Barricade, and Regroup cost 1 energy Must

The card data for Listen, Barricade, and Regroup sets an energy cost of 1 each. All other existing cards remain at the default cost of 0.

Lifecycle: gain, carry-over, reset

REQ-ENERGY-4 Energy initializes to 0 at world start, before the first turn's gain Must

When a world is created, energy starts at 0. The start-of-turn gain (REQ-ENERGY-5) then applies to the opening turn, so the player begins turn 1 with 1 energy and can afford one cost-1 card immediately.

Concretely: the opening hand deal is a turn start and triggers the +1, exactly as every subsequent turn does. There is no special-casing of turn 1 beyond the initial value of 0.

Implementation site: createWorld builds its bootstrap GameState inline and currently has no per-turn hook. The bootstrap must initialize energy: 0, and the start-of-turn gain (REQ-ENERGY-5) must run for the opening hand so the returned state has energy === 1. The gain belongs in a shared step invoked by both createWorld and the EndTurn handler, not duplicated in two places (see REQ-ENERGY-5 for ordering relative to refillHand).

REQ-ENERGY-5 +1 energy at the start of every turn Must

At the start of each turn, energy increases by exactly 1. "Start of turn" is the same boundary at which the hand is refilled: the opening hand at world creation, and after each EndTurn. The gain happens once per turn boundary.

The gain is unconditional. It is not suppressed by skipDrawNext or any other flag, and it is not tied to whether any cards are actually drawn.

Ordering: the +1 is applied to GameState.energy before refillHand runs, so any effect that reads energy during the turn-start sequence sees the post-gain value. A single shared "gain energy" step is the only place energy increments per turn.

REQ-ENERGY-6 Unspent energy carries over between turns, with no cap Must

Energy is never reset or reduced at the end of a turn. Whatever the player did not spend carries into the next turn and accumulates with that turn's gain. There is no maximum; energy may grow without bound (e.g. a player who never spends reaches energy N on turn N).

REQ-ENERGY-7 Energy resets only at world start, never on act transitions Must

Energy is reset to 0 only when a new world is created. It persists unchanged across act transitions within a world (the ActAdvanced boundary does not touch energy). Advancing from act 1 to act 2 carries the player's current energy forward.

World-to-world transitions (the win path, where the next world is built via a fresh createWorld) reset energy to 0 by definition of REQ-ENERGY-4. Only act-internal boundaries preserve it.

Spending & the cost gate

REQ-ENERGY-8 A card is affordable only when energy ≥ its energy cost Must

A player card may be played only when current energy is greater than or equal to its energy cost. Affordability is an additional gate layered on top of the existing playability rules (target availability, etc.): a card must satisfy both its structural requirements and the energy requirement to be playable.

Cost-0 cards are always affordable and behave exactly as they do today.

REQ-ENERGY-9 Unaffordable cards are excluded from available actions Must

A card the player cannot afford does not appear in availableActions().playable. The selector is the single source of truth for what the UI may offer, so an unaffordable card is simply not offered.

REQ-ENERGY-10 Playing a card deducts its energy cost Must

When a player card is successfully played, energy decreases by that card's energy cost. The deduction is part of resolving the play. After playing a cost-1 card, energy is exactly 1 lower than before; playing a cost-0 card leaves energy unchanged.

Energy must never go negative. Because the affordability gate (REQ-ENERGY-8) is enforced before deduction, a legal play can always pay its cost in full.

REQ-ENERGY-11 Illegal (unaffordable) plays are rejected by the reducer Must

If a PlayCard action for an unaffordable card reaches the reducer, it is rejected with IllegalActionError (the same failure mode as any other illegal play). The reducer never silently no-ops or partially applies the effect.

REQ-ENERGY-12 Energy cost is per-card, independent of modal/sequence branch Should

A card's energy cost is a single value for the whole card. For cards whose effect is a Modal or Sequence, the cost does not vary by chosen branch or step: the same cost is required to play the card and is deducted once, regardless of which branch the player selects.

(None of the three costed cards in this spec are modal; this requirement fixes the rule so future costed cards are unambiguous.)

Loss detection & events

REQ-ENERGY-13 Energy gating must not trigger a false loss Must

The existing livelock loss guards in EndTurn declare a loss when no progress is possible. Because affordability now removes cards from playable, a card that is unaffordable only because energy is currently too low must not be treated as permanently dead: energy regenerates +1 every turn with no cap, so such a card becomes affordable in a finite number of turns.

Therefore the loss guards must not declare WorldLost in a state where the only thing standing between the player and a legal play is insufficient energy. A player who can reach a legal play by ending turns to bank energy is not in a lost state.

Per-guard analysis (reduce.ts has two loss guards at EndTurn):

  • Guard A (all piles exhausted, then "no progress possible" via availableActions().playable and discardable) is affected: affordability now removes cards from playable, so a costed-but-soon-affordable card could make this guard falsely conclude no progress. This guard must treat a card that is unaffordable only due to current energy as future progress, not as dead.
  • Guard B (noWorldAnywhere and no player card with AddWorldCardToTop) is not affected: it tests for the existence of an AddWorldCardToTop card across all player zones, not its current playability or affordability. Energy gating never changes a card's existence, so guard B needs no change. This spec records that conclusion so the implementer does not re-derive it.
REQ-ENERGY-14 Energy changes emit an observable event Should

Changes to energy (gain at turn start, spend on play) emit an EnergyChanged game event carrying the new energy total ({ type: 'EnergyChanged'; energy: number }), mirroring HpChanged. This lets the renderer animate or react to energy changes without diffing whole states.

Presentation

REQ-ENERGY-15 The HUD surfaces current energy Must

The HUD displays the player's current energy alongside HP and act status, updating in step with GameState. The exact label and styling are a view-layer detail; the requirement is that the player can always see how much energy they have.

REQ-ENERGY-16 A card's energy cost is prominently displayed on the card Must

Cards with a non-zero energy cost display that cost prominently on the card face: a dedicated cost indicator (e.g. a corner cost badge) that reads as a primary attribute of the card, not buried in description text. The player must be able to tell a card's cost at a glance, before committing to play it.

Cost-0 cards need not display a cost. The exact visual treatment (placement, shape, color) is a view-layer detail; what is mandated is that a non-zero cost is present and prominent.

✅ AI Validation

How the AI verifies this is done. Each check is behavioral and runnable against the pure core (and the view where noted). All core checks should be expressed as unit tests in src/core/tests/; presentation checks in src/game/tests/ or manual browser observation.

REQ-1,2,3 Load each relevant world source separately (Listen/Regroup from zombie-big-box.json, Barricade from starter.json) and confirm each resolves to a player card with energy cost 1. Confirm at least one other player card resolves to energy cost 0 (default applied when data omits the field).
REQ-4 Assert the return value of createWorld has energy === 1 (bootstrap initial 0 plus the turn-1 gain applied before the opening hand is dealt).
REQ-5,6 From a known state, dispatch EndTurn with no plays and assert energy increased by exactly 1. Repeat several turns and assert monotonic +1 with no cap (e.g. energy reaches 5 after 4 additional end-turns from energy 1).
REQ-5 Set skipDrawNext true, end the turn, and assert energy still gained +1 (gain is independent of the draw-skip).
REQ-6,7 Drive the game to an act transition (ActAdvanced in the returned events), capturing state.energy immediately before and after, and assert the value is identical across the boundary (act transition never resets energy). Separately, end a turn and assert energy incremented by exactly 1 — together these show energy changes only via the turn-start gain, not act boundaries.
REQ-8,9 Construct a state with energy 0 and a cost-1 card (Listen) in hand whose structural requirements are otherwise met; assert it is absent from availableActions().playable. Raise energy to 1 and assert it now appears.
REQ-10 With energy 1 and a cost-1 card legally played, assert state.energy === 0 afterward, and that energy never reads negative at any step.
REQ-11 With energy 0, dispatch a PlayCard for a cost-1 card directly to the reducer and assert it throws IllegalActionError and leaves state unchanged.
REQ-12 Add a synthetic test fixture: a costed card whose effect is a Modal/Sequence (none of the three shipping costed cards are modal). Assert the same cost is deducted exactly once regardless of the chosen branch. If no such fixture is added, mark this check explicitly deferred rather than silently passing.
REQ-13 Construct an end-of-turn state where the only player card that could make progress is a costed card currently unaffordable, with energy that will rise next turn, and assert the game does not transition to lost / emit WorldLost.
REQ-14 Assert an energy-change event with the new total is present in the returned events on a turn-start gain and on a costed play.
REQ-15 Unit-test the HUD updater shows the current energy value; manually confirm in the browser that the energy readout updates on gain and spend.
REQ-16 Unit-test that a card object built for a cost-1 card (Listen/Barricade/Regroup) includes a distinct cost indicator element, and a cost-0 card does not.
