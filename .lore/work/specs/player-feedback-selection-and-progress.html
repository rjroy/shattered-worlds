Spec: target clarity before commit and partial hazard progress

Two UX-feedback concerns from the deckbuilder table, specced as a delta over the shipped targeting renderer · derived from the spec-ready brainstorm

Framing. This is feedback design, not a mechanics change. The targeting grammar (targeting-interaction.html) and the progress/reset rules (world-deck-slice.html, REQ-WDS-9/10) already ship and stay unchanged. Every requirement here is renderer-side: how the player reads what they are about to do and how close a hazard is to clearing. Nothing in this spec may alter GameState or core reducers.

Concern 1 — Target clarity before commit

The player is mid-targeting and must read, at a glance and before the click, which hazard the hit will land on and what it will do. Q5 kept click-to-commit (no confirm beat), so the entire safety margin is the hover read. Wrong-hazard commit is the worst case, so the loud highlight, connector, decoration, and hover timing (FEEDBACK-1/2/3/4) are the load-bearing requirements; keeping earlier picks lit (FEEDBACK-6) is secondary.
REQ-FEEDBACK-1 Loud, unmistakable target highlight must manual

The hovered legal target during a targeting phase must be the loudest read on the board, distinct on its own rather than by comparison to other cards. The "I am the target" state must go beyond a border tint (the only signal today) and add at least one of: scale/lift, a glow, or an on-card reticle. It must be visibly different from the merely-legal-but-unhovered target state and from the dimmed non-target state.

Why & seams

Today the target read is carried by color difference alone: applyCardHighlight(container,'target',fs) sets a 3px frameStyle.targetBorder stroke (render.ts:374-376), and everything else dims to dimAlpha 0.35 (render.ts:60,387). Targeted vs legal-but-unselected differ only by which cards hold which border color. The brainstorm's Q5 makes this the safety margin: with no confirm step, an ambiguous hover read is a wrong-hazard commit waiting to happen. The new emphasis applies to the hovered target specifically, layered on the existing applyHighlight path (TableScene.ts:308-368).

REQ-FEEDBACK-2 Connector from acting card to hovered target must manual

While a target is hovered, a literal connector (line or arrow) must be drawn from the acting card to that target. Its job is to name the target: it replaces "bind two same-time border colors in your head" with "follow the line to the thing about to get hit." The connector appears on hover-over of a legal target and is removed on hover-out or commit.

Why & seams

No connector geometry exists anywhere in src/game today (no lineBetween / line primitive), so this is greenfield. It hangs off the existing hover hooks (TableScene.ts:300-301: pointerover → showTargetPreview, pointerout → updateHint). The source endpoint is the selected acting card (sel.cardId); the target endpoint is the hovered legal target. Must respect Phaser depth so the line draws above dimmed cards without eating their pointer events (see the depth-sort / input-block lessons in visual-identity-rollout.html).

REQ-FEEDBACK-3 Connector decorated by targeting type must unit + manual

The connector must carry what the action does, not just where it lands, using a small, consistent, learnable vocabulary of three styles selected from the acting card's effect against the target:

  • Progress → accent-colored line feeding into the target's cost ring (FEEDBACK-7).
  • Destroy → harsh / jagged red line.
  • Back to deck (return) → curved arrow looping toward the deck.

The mapping from action to style must be a pure function so it is unit-testable independent of Phaser. The three styles correspond to three distinct targeting phases, not one: the connector draws from the acting card to the hovered target regardless of whether that target is a hazard, a returned world card, or a destroyed hand card.

Why & seams

This makes the link do double duty: disambiguate the target (Concern 1) and pre-read the effect, so the player is not guessing whether the hit chips, kills, or bounces the hazard. The effect kind and selection phase are both discoverable: DealProgress → awaiting-hazard (see dealProgressOf, describe.ts:112+); DestroyCardInHand → awaiting-destroy (targets a hand card, e.g. the Zombie Big-Box destroy effect); ReturnWorldCards → awaiting-return (targets world cards, e.g. Barricade / Adrenaline). All three phases exist in selection.ts. Keep the style-selection logic out of the render call so it can be tested as data → style.

REQ-FEEDBACK-4 Highlight and connector render on hover, before click must manual

Because the game stays click-to-commit with no confirm step (brainstorm Q5), both the loud highlight (FEEDBACK-1) and the connector (FEEDBACK-2/3) must appear on hover of a legal target, before any click. The hover state is the only thing standing between the player and a wrong-target commit; nothing about the target read may depend on a held post-selection beat that does not exist.

Why & seams

showTargetPreview(targetId) already fires on pointerover during awaiting-hazard and no-ops for illegal targets (TableScene.ts:662-677). The new highlight/connector hook into the same hover entry/exit so they live and die with the pointer, matching the preview's lifecycle.

REQ-FEEDBACK-5 Instruction hint and live preview must not clobber each other must manual

The targeting instruction ("Select a Hazard target") and the live hover preview ("Deals 2 → 1 more to clear") must occupy distinct surfaces so one does not overwrite the other. Today a single bottom-center slot carries both.

Why & seams

this.selectionHint is shared: updateHint() writes phase instructions (TableScene.ts:679-706) and showTargetPreview() writes the preview into the same setText target (:676). On pointerout the preview is cleared by re-running updateHint(). The fix is a second text/preview surface (e.g. anchored near the hovered target or a dedicated preview slot) so instruction and preview coexist. The preview string itself stays sourced from previewPlay (single source of truth; honor self-describing-card-faces.html). The targeting design requires affordances to work on touch as well as mouse, so a pointer-following floating slot must degrade gracefully when there is no hover (touch): the preview may appear on the target's own tap-to-preview, but the two surfaces must never collapse back into one shared slot.

REQ-FEEDBACK-6 Keep earlier picks lit through multi-step plays should manual

During a compound play's later step, the hazard target already chosen by an earlier step must stay visibly marked, so the player can still see which hazard the earlier step is committed to. Secondary to the single-step read (FEEDBACK-1/2/3): wrong-hazard commit was ranked the worse failure, so this is a SHOULD, but the read still must not go fully dark mid-sequence. The committed-earlier-step target must carry a persistent "committed" mark that is visually distinct from both the active hover highlight (FEEDBACK-1) and the legal-but-unhovered state — a muted, steady version of the target emphasis reads as "already locked here," not "hover me." (Only Barricade produces a compound flow in the current catalog, so this requirement has a single live trigger today.)

Why & seams

applyHighlight re-lights awaiting-return selections (TableScene.ts:349-354) and current legal targets, but nothing re-lights a stored earlier hazard targetId once the flow advances past it. The renderer would need to track the earlier-committed target id(s) for the in-flight compound and apply the persistent "committed target" mark.

Concern 2 — Partial hazard progress

A hazard can take some progress this turn without clearing, and every point evaporates at end of turn (REQ-WDS-10; handleEndTurn sets progress:{} at reduce.ts:129). The player needs three reads at a glance: banked this turn, remaining to clear, and it resets if I don't finish. A static bar teaches the first two and hides the third, so the drain animation (FEEDBACK-9) is core teaching, not polish.
REQ-FEEDBACK-7 Cost-ring progress meter on the world card face must unit + manual

The hazard card face must show accumulated progress as a ring wrapping the existing cost digit. The ring's filled fraction equals min(progress, cost) / cost. The big cost number stays readable inside the ring. The meter must read cleanly in the common case (cost 1–2) and survive the single boss at cost 10 (The Walker) without changing shape, which is why a ring beats a pip row (brainstorm Q2). The fill-fraction computation must be a pure, unit-testable function.

Why & seams

Accumulated progress is drawn nowhere on the card today: the face shows name, cost digit + "to clear" label (render.ts:130-142), keywords, and effect lines; state.progress[cardId] only surfaces in the one-line hover preview (previewPlay, describe.ts:103). The cost-ring is net-new surface on the world card face. Reads from state.progress[cardId] ?? 0 and worldCard.cost — no new state. Ring color for the progress-feeding line (FEEDBACK-3) should pair with this ring's accent. When progress reaches cost the hazard resolves (HazardResolved) and the card leaves the board on its own exit animation; the ring is not expected to play a separate "cleared" beat — reaching full and the card exiting is the resolution read. No drain (FEEDBACK-9) fires for a resolved hazard, only for one still held at end of turn.

REQ-FEEDBACK-8 Ring fills as progress is banked this turn must manual

When progress lands on a hazard, the ring must visibly fill toward the new fraction rather than snapping. Together with the drain (FEEDBACK-9) this is one consistent clock: it fills as you bank and empties at end of turn, telling the whole burst-or-lose story in one visual. The fill and the drain should share a comparable, clearly perceptible animation duration (roughly 200–400ms) so they read as the same clock running in two directions.

REQ-FEEDBACK-9 End-of-turn drain animation (core teaching beat) must manual

At end of turn, any hazard holding partial progress must visibly drain back to zero rather than snap. This is treated as core mechanic-teaching (brainstorm Q1/Q3: the per-turn reset is intended and meant to be felt and managed), not polish. Watching banked progress bleed away once should make the burst-timing pressure legible without a tooltip.

Why & seams

The reset happens in handleEndTurn: world cards stay in hand (reduce.ts:127) and progress is wiped to {} (:129), emitting a TurnEnded event (:107). The renderer must animate the drain on the held hazards between the old progress value and zero, driven off the turn-end event rather than a silent state swap. Because the card persists across the reset, the same ring object animates down. Source of the pre-reset value: TurnEnded carries no progress snapshot, so the renderer is responsible for caching the last-known progress per held hazard; on TurnEnded it reads the cache, tweens the ring to zero, then discards the cache. This is renderer-side state (consistent with FEEDBACK-11); the event payload stays unchanged.

Cross-cutting constraints

REQ-FEEDBACK-10 Progress reads from a single source of truth must unit

Everything that senses "how close is this hazard to clearing" must derive from the same data (state.progress + cost, surfaced through the existing describe/preview layer): the ring meter (FEEDBACK-7), the hover preview and any "X more to clear" text (FEEDBACK-5), and the progress-vs-other-effect decision that selects the connector style (FEEDBACK-3). No second progress computation may be introduced that could drift from previewPlay. This is a cross-cutting constraint because both concerns read progress: Concern 2 to fill the ring, Concern 1 to know the hit is a progress hit and to preview its result.

Why & seams

Honors the self-describing-card-faces.html retro conclusion: card-text and preview already flow through a single describe layer. The ring fraction (FEEDBACK-7), the preview string (previewPlay, describe.ts:91-110), and the connector-style resolver (FEEDBACK-3) must agree by construction — two code paths computing "how close to clearing" differently is a known bug source.

REQ-FEEDBACK-11 All new feedback is renderer-only must structural

No requirement in this spec may add to or change GameState, the core reducers, or any pure-core module. Connector geometry, the cost ring, the highlight emphasis, and the drain animation all live in src/game (the thin renderer). Selection remains renderer-only transient state per targeting-interaction.html and core-render-architecture.html.

REQ-FEEDBACK-12 Juice and colors hook the existing theme / intensity system must manual

Connector colors, highlight glow, ring accent, and any intensity-scaled emphasis must draw from the existing VisualTheme.frameStyle and the intensity signal (visual-identity.html), not a parallel hardcoded palette. Theme-supplied colors already exist for borders (frameStyle.selectedBorder/targetBorder/discardBorder, theme.ts:5-7); new feedback surfaces extend that set rather than inventing one.

Why & seams

The targeting design explicitly says highlight/commit juice should be intensity-scaled and renderer-owned. New theme fields (e.g. a connector palette keyed by targeting type, a ring accent) belong in FrameStyle / VisualTheme so every world theme can restyle them, consistent with the visual-identity seam.

Out of scope (deferred)

Captured so the thinking is not lost; explicitly not required by this spec.

  • "Can't finish this turn" pre-warning — marking a hazard when the remaining hand mathematically cannot cover the gap. Deferred; revisit as a follow-on once the ring + connector ship.
  • Pip-row meter — the ring was chosen as the single meter (Q2); a pip alternative is redundant.
  • Floating RPG damage numbers on the target — reads as result, not intent; revisit only if highlight + link underdeliver.
  • Progressive cracked / broken hazard art states — expensive, does not scale to data-driven cards, gives no exact number. Maybe a single hero hazard later.
  • Acting-card feedback (action tray, modal-branch echo) — answers "which card / which branch am I playing," a different concern from "which target will this hit."

AI Validation

How the AI verifies this spec is done. Pure-function requirements are gated by unit tests the AI runs; the perceptual requirements (a highlight being "loud," a drain being "visible") require launching the game in a browser and observing, the same manual-check pattern used for REQ-VIS-18 in visual-identity.html. Both paths are listed per requirement below.

Automated gate (AI runs these)

  • bun test — full suite green, including new unit tests below. No existing test (currently 149) regresses.
  • Typecheck and lint clean (tsc / eslint), no new warnings — warnings are never suppressed.
  • New unit tests required:
    • Ring fill fraction (FEEDBACK-7): pure fn returns 0 at progress 0, 0.5 at 1/2, 1.0 at progress≥cost, and clamps (progress 3, cost 2 → 1.0); correct for the cost-10 boss case.
    • Connector style selection (FEEDBACK-3): pure fn maps a DealProgress action → progress style, a destroy action → destroy style, a return action → back-to-deck style.
    • Single-source agreement (FEEDBACK-10): for a sampled set of (card, target, progress) cases, the ring fraction and previewPlay's "more to clear" number describe the same remaining amount (ring reaches full exactly when the preview says "clears").
    • No core surface change (FEEDBACK-11): this is a structural check, not a behavioral unit test — an import-boundary / grep assertion that the feature adds nothing to GameState or src/core (no new progress/selection fields in core types, no new core imports from src/game). Satisfied by CI lint + a type-definition grep, not by a unit test.
