Spec · First Real Gameplay

The world deck is the game

One playable world, single-currency Progress vs Hazard, where the world deck is both the threat and where you acquire cards. Concrete content: Zombie Invasion at the Big-Box Store. Built on the proven core/render seam from the POC.

◇ Purpose & the loop

The POC proved the architecture with a trivial draw/sum loop. This slice replaces that loop with the real game: the mechanics resolved across Progress vs Hazard and the world deck as acquisition engine. It is the first build that is meant to be fun, and the cheap playable artifact the vision asks for before deep engineering (Principle 7). It stays one world, one hardcoded run; Destiny, between-run economy, and multiple worlds are explicitly out of scope.

New world (shuffle Act 1, deal opening hand)→ Play player cards → deal Progress at Hazards→ Hazards resolve (reward) / discard (penalty) / hold→ End Turn (discard player hand, world cards stay, redraw)↺ Win: resolve a Door · Lose: HP 0

Resolved decisions & assumptions (veto any of these)

  • Starting HP = 20. A tuning dial, expected to move with playtest data (Principle 6), not a fixed truth. (Assumed.)
  • Act advancement happens when the current act's world draw pile is exhausted: the next act's cards become the new world draw pile. Unresolved world cards in hand persist across the boundary. (Assumed.)
  • A Hazard auto-resolves the instant its Progress-paid-this-turn reaches its cost. There is no separate "resolve" action; resolution is the emergent result of dealing enough Progress. (Assumed.)
  • Within a turn, multiple cards stack Progress on one Hazard; across turns that partial payment resets to 0 (the no-carry-over rule). This is what makes the 10-cost Walker demand a single burst turn. (Confirmed in notes.)
  • Answer cards target one Hazard in hand; a keyword bonus (e.g. Physical vs Creature) checks that target. (Assumed from card text.)
  • End of turn: unplayed player cards discard; world cards cannot be discarded by EndTurn and persist in hand. (Confirmed with user.)
  • "Survive the world" is a win screen. No reward draft, no Destiny, no next world in this slice. The Walker may be undefeatable on this deck; surviving via the forced Door is success, not a bug. (Confirmed in notes.)
  • One hardcoded world and a fixed/seeded setup; no world-select UI. Multipliers like x5 Progress are out of scope here. (Assumed.)
A · Architecture continuity
REQ-WDS-1All new rules live in the pure, synchronous, seeded coreMust

Every mechanic in this spec is implemented under src/core/ with zero Phaser/DOM/canvas, extending the existing dispatch(action) → { state, events } seam from the approved architecture. Dispatch stays synchronous and pure: same prior state + action ⇒ identical next state and events. All randomness (act shuffles, return-to-top-3 placement) flows through the seeded PRNG already in GameState. The lint import-boundary rule continues to fail the build if the core imports Phaser or src/game/.

REQ-WDS-2State is immutable; card instances are uniquely identified and accounted forMust

Each dispatch yields a new snapshot; the prior is never mutated. Every card instance (player or world) carries a unique stable id. At all times a given id exists in exactly one place — a zone, or removed from the game — and every removal or creation is explained by an emitted event. Unlike the POC's fixed ten-card total, this slice creates cards (rewards) and removes them (resolve, discard, destroy); the invariant is no duplication and no silent loss, not a constant count.

B · Domain model
REQ-WDS-3Player cards are effect-bearing, not bare valuesMust

A player card carries an id, a name, and an effect drawn from a small vocabulary sufficient to express the starter deck and reward cards (REQ-WDS-23/24): DealProgress (with optional keyword bonus and a target Hazard), Draw (player and/or world), Heal, ReturnWorldCards, DestroyCardInHand, DiscardThenDraw, AddCard (a player card to discard or top of player draw pile), AddWorldCardToTop (a world card to the top of the world draw pile — this is Summon Door's effect), and a Modal wrapper (choose one of N sub-effects). The vocabulary is a closed enum for this slice, not a general scripting engine.

REQ-WDS-4World cards (Hazards) model cost, keywords, penalty, and rewardMust

A world card carries an id, a name, an integer Hazard cost, a set of keyword tags, a discardable flag, a penalty (applied on discard), and an optional reward (granted on resolve). Penalty and reward are each drawn from a closed vocabulary: penalties include Damage(n), SkipDrawNextTurn, GainCard(card), AddWorldCardToTop(card), and None; rewards include GainCard(card), AddPlayerCardToTop(card), SurviveWorld, and None. A card with discardable = false (the Door) cannot be targeted by DiscardHazard at all; this is a guard on the action, separate from the penalty vocabulary.

REQ-WDS-5Keyword tags drive conditional Progress bonuses (single currency)Must

Keywords are plain tags on cards: Hidden, Creature, Slow on Hazards; Explore N and Physical N on player effects. A bonus keyword means "deal N extra Progress when the targeted Hazard has the matching tag." There are no separate sub-currencies: every bonus resolves to the one Progress number, which is what keeps multipliers and totals legible.

REQ-WDS-6GameState models zones, HP, per-turn Hazard progress, and actsMust

GameState represents: the player draw pile / hand / discard; the world draw pile (the current act, ordered) and the remaining acts queue; the hand holds both player and world cards (world cards held there are the clog); per-Hazard Progress-paid-this-turn; the player's HP; a skip-draw flag for the next turn; a status (playing / won / lost); and the seeded PRNG cursor. Resolved, discarded, and destroyed cards leave the game (they are not retained in a zone).

C · The turn loop
REQ-WDS-7Draw refills toward six, always taking at least one world cardMust

Each turn the hand is refilled toward a size of 6. New world draws = max(1, 2 − heldWorld), capped by world cards remaining and by room in the hand; new player draws then fill the remainder up to 6. The familiar cases:

Player draws may legitimately reach 0 when enough world cards are held (e.g. holding 5 → 0 player + 1 world; the hand can choke, which is the intended spiral). When the hand is already at 6 from held world cards, no cards are drawn at all (the minimum-one-world rule applies only when there is room). If the player draw pile empties mid-draw, the player discard is reshuffled (seeded) into a new draw pile (emits DeckShuffled), as in the POC. The skip-draw penalty (Rubble) suppresses player draws for one turn; the minimum world draw still applies.

REQ-WDS-8Playing a player card applies its effect; answer cards deal Progress at a target HazardMust

PlayCard{ cardId, targetId?, choice? } moves the card from hand and applies its effect. For a DealProgress effect, targetId names a Hazard currently in hand; the dealt Progress is base + (bonus if the target carries the matching keyword) and is added to that Hazard's Progress-paid-this-turn. Effects requiring a selection carry it in the action: Modal uses choice (e.g. Sprint's draw-vs-deal), ReturnWorldCards and DestroyCardInHand carry the target id(s). A DealProgress card requires at least one Hazard in hand as a target and is unplayable when none exists (dispatching it then throws); it never deals Progress "to the void." Only legal targets/choices are accepted; an illegal action (unknown card, wrong-type or absent target, out-of-range modal choice, targeting a player card where a world card is required) throws a typed error carrying the action and state (the POC error contract, unchanged).

REQ-WDS-9A Hazard resolves the instant its turn-Progress meets its costMust

Immediately after any effect raises a Hazard's Progress-paid-this-turn to ≥ its cost, that Hazard resolves: its reward is granted (REQ-WDS-14), it leaves the hand, and a HazardResolved event is emitted. Excess Progress beyond the cost is wasted (not redistributed). Resolution is automatic and within the same dispatch as the effect that triggered it.

REQ-WDS-10Progress never carries between turnsMust

Progress is not a stored pool; it exists only as per-Hazard payment accrued during the current turn. At EndTurn every Hazard's Progress-paid-this-turn resets to 0. A Hazard partially paid and then held must be paid again from scratch next turn. This is intentional and load-bearing (it is why big Hazards require a burst).

REQ-WDS-11Discarding a Hazard applies its penalty and is always legalMust

DiscardHazard{ cardId } applies that Hazard's penalty (deal damage, set the skip-draw flag, gain a card, or add a world card to the top of the world deck), removes it from hand, and emits HazardDiscarded plus a penalty event. It is legal at any time during the player's turn, on any world card in hand except those whose penalty is cannot-be-discarded (the Door); attempting to discard such a card throws.

REQ-WDS-12Held world cards persist; unplayed player cards do notMust

Doing nothing with a world card ("hold") leaves it in hand into the next turn, where it reduces the player draw per REQ-WDS-7. By contrast, player cards do not persist: any unplayed player card is discarded at end of turn. Holding is the only way a card stays in hand across the turn boundary, and only world cards can do it.

REQ-WDS-13EndTurn discards the player hand, resets Progress, and redrawsMust

EndTurn, in strict order: (1) move every player card remaining in hand to the player discard (world cards stay); (2) reset every Hazard's Progress-paid-this-turn to 0; (3) consume the skip-draw flag if set; (4) draw per REQ-WDS-7. Steps (1)–(3) complete before (4). Emits an ordered sequence (TurnEnded, CardsDiscarded, optional DeckShuffled/ActAdvanced, CardsDrawn) with the specific card ids moved.

D · Acquisition economy
REQ-WDS-14Rewards land in the player discard by defaultMust

A GainCard reward (or penalty) adds a new card instance, with a fresh unique id, to the player discard pile by default, so it shuffles into play later. A reward may override the destination: AddPlayerCardToTop places it on top of the player draw pile (Summon Door), and AddWorldCardToTop places a world card on top of the world draw pile (the Walker's penalty Door). Every gain emits a CardGained event naming the new id and destination.

REQ-WDS-15Returned world cards shuffle into the top three of the world deckMust

ReturnWorldCards (Barricade up to 2, Panic exactly 1) removes the named world cards from hand and inserts them into the top three positions of the current world draw pile, ordered by the seeded PRNG. The named ids must be world cards in hand; naming a player card or a card not in hand throws (REQ-WDS-8). Returning grants no reward and triggers no penalty; the card simply re-enters the deck and may be drawn again soon. Returning 0 is valid for Barricade; Panic requires exactly 1 world card in hand and is unplayable otherwise. A returned card cannot itself be a card with discardable = false (the Door is never returnable, as no effect names it). Emits WorldCardsReturned.

REQ-WDS-16Regroup permanently destroys a card in hand (thinning)Must

The Regroup card's DestroyCardInHand effect removes up to one chosen card in hand from the game permanently (not to any discard). It may target a clogging world card or a junk player card. Destroying 0 is valid. Emits CardDestroyed. This is the slice's deck-thinning lever (research: thinning is a core strategy).

REQ-WDS-17Penalties resolve against HP, draw, and the decks as specifiedMust

On discard, a Hazard's penalty applies exactly: Damage(n) subtracts n from HP (and may trigger loss, REQ-WDS-21); SkipDrawNextTurn sets the skip-draw flag (a boolean: suppresses player draws on the next turn only; if already set, a further SkipDrawNextTurn is a no-op — it does not stack or count multiple turns); GainCard adds a card per REQ-WDS-14; AddWorldCardToTop places a world card on top of the world draw pile; None does nothing. Each emits a specific penalty event.

E · Acts & the world
REQ-WDS-18The world is ordered acts; advancement empties one act into the nextMust

A world is a fixed ordered list of acts, each a set of world cards. createWorld(seed) shuffles Act 1 into the world draw pile and queues the later acts in order. When the world draw pile is exhausted and a draw is required, the next act is shuffled to become the new world draw pile and an ActAdvanced event is emitted; drawing continues. This applies to any world draw, whether at EndTurn or triggered mid-turn by a card effect (Sprint's draw mode, Panic): a Hazard drawn mid-turn enters the hand immediately and is targetable that same turn. Held/unresolved world cards in hand are unaffected by the boundary. If no act remains and the world pile is empty, no further world card is drawn (the minimum-one rule yields nothing rather than crashing), so play continues toward a terminal state.

REQ-WDS-20The Walker chain produces a Door, and resolving a Door wins the worldMust

Summon Door (player card, granted by resolving The Walker): its effect adds a Door to the top of the world draw pile. Door (world card): cost 2, penalty cannot-be-discarded, reward SurviveWorld. Both paths reach a Door: resolving The Walker grants Summon Door (player-timed), discarding it forces a Door directly (REQ-WDS-17). The Door has discardable = false, so it cannot be discarded; it may be held across turns and acts, and the only way it leaves the hand is by resolving it (paying its 2 Progress). Holding it indefinitely is legal but never wins; the world is won only when the Door is resolved.

F · Win & loss
REQ-WDS-21Win by resolving a Door; lose when HP reaches zeroMust

Granting a SurviveWorld reward sets status to won and emits WorldWon. HP reaching 0 or below at any point sets status to lost and emits WorldLost. Once status is not playing, further gameplay actions are rejected (throw). There is no score target and no turn limit.

REQ-WDS-22The Walker need not be defeatable on the starter deckShould

Because Progress does not carry over, resolving the 10-cost Walker on the starter deck in a single turn is expected to be impossible, and that is intended (it is the wall that motivates future meta-progression). Surviving the world via the forced Door without ever resolving The Walker is a legitimate win, not a softlock or bug. The slice must reach a win this way.

G · The card catalog
REQ-WDS-23The starter deck is exactly ten cards with these effectsMust
  • Sprint ×2 — Modal with two branches: (a) draw 2 player cards and 1 world card; or (b) deal +1 Progress to a Slow Hazard. Branch (b) requires a Slow Hazard target and is unavailable when none is in hand; branch (a) is always available, so Sprint is never a dead card.
  • Explore ×3 — deal +1 Progress to a target Hazard; Explore 1 (+1 more vs Hidden).
  • Barricade ×2 — deal +1 Progress to a target Hazard, then return up to 2 world cards to the world deck (0 valid). Requires a Hazard target like any DealProgress card (REQ-WDS-8).
  • Med Kit ×1 — heal +2 HP.
  • Panic ×1 — return 1 world card to the world deck, then draw 1 world card. Requires exactly 1 world card in hand (REQ-WDS-15).
  • Adrenaline ×1 — discard 1 player card, then draw 2 player cards (it never draws world cards). Unplayable if no player card is in hand to discard.
REQ-WDS-24Reward and gained cards have these effectsMust
  • Listen — deal +1 Progress to a target Hazard; Explore 2 (+2 more vs Hidden).
  • Baseball Bat — deal +2 Progress to a target Hazard; Physical 3 (+3 more vs Creature).
  • Regroup — destroy up to 1 card in hand (permanent).
  • Summon Door — add a Door to the top of the world draw pile.
  • Panic / Adrenaline — the same cards as in the starter deck, gained as copies.
H · Events & renderer
REQ-WDS-25The event log is animation-sufficient and carries ids and amountsShould

Each dispatch returns an ordered semantic event list covering at least: CardPlayed, ProgressDealt{ hazardId, amount, hazardTurnTotal }, HazardResolved{ id }, HazardDiscarded{ id }, penalty events (DamageDealt, DrawSkipped), CardGained{ id, dest }, CardDestroyed{ id }, WorldCardsReturned{ ids }, HpChanged{ hp }, CardsDiscarded, DeckShuffled, ActAdvanced{ act }, CardsDrawn{ ids }, TurnEnded, WorldWon, WorldLost. Card-moving events carry the specific ids so the renderer animates from the log without diffing states.

REQ-WDS-26The core exposes a derived intensity signalShould

Per the architecture's escalation seam, the core exposes a pure intensity read-model computed from run state (e.g. current act, HP fraction, count of held Hazards). The renderer may read it to scale juice. The exact curve is not fixed here; only that the signal is derived in the core, not guessed in the renderer.

REQ-WDS-27A Phaser renderer makes the world playableMust

A Phaser scene observes core state and displays: the hand with player and world cards visually distinct; each Hazard's cost and current turn-Progress, keywords, penalty, and reward; the player's HP; the current act and world/player pile counts; and an End Turn button. The player can play a card (choosing a target Hazard or modal option where required), discard a Hazard, and end the turn, each dispatching the matching action. The renderer reflects the resulting state and shows a win and a loss screen. Visual polish is not required; clear, correct interaction is. The targeting and selection interaction (how a card is selected, how legal targets are highlighted, discard zones, the cancel/confirm flow) is designed in targeting-interaction.

That design adds one shared core surface to honor here: a pure availableActions(state) selector in src/core/ that returns which cards are playable, each card's target requirements, and which Hazards are discardable. It is the single source of legality for three consumers — the renderer's highlights, this slice's dispatch validation (REQ-WDS-8), and the sim's legal-action policy (REQ-WDS-29) — so targeting rules are never duplicated across them.

I · Testing, sim & CI
REQ-WDS-28The core is unit-tested headlessly and gated in CIMust

Core tests run with no Phaser/DOM/canvas and cover: world composition (REQ-WDS-19), deterministic act shuffle and return-to-top-3 from seed, the draw table (REQ-WDS-7) including held-world reduction and skip-draw, Progress dealing with keyword bonuses (REQ-WDS-5/8), auto-resolution at cost and no-carry-over reset (REQ-WDS-9/10), each penalty and reward (REQ-WDS-14/17), Regroup/return/discard, act advancement (REQ-WDS-18), the Walker→Door win path (REQ-WDS-20/22), the loss path at 0 HP, the id-accounting invariant (REQ-WDS-2), and replay equivalence (same seed + actions ⇒ identical state & events). CI runs the import-boundary lint and the suite on every change.

REQ-WDS-29The headless sim runner drives the new loop to a terminal stateShould

The sim runner uses only the core and a random-legal policy over the new action set (play a legal card with a legal target/choice, discard a discardable Hazard, or end the turn). It runs many full worlds at full speed, each reaching won or lost without throwing and preserving the id-accounting invariant throughout, and records simple metrics (turns taken, end HP, win/loss, cards acquired). This proves the one-core-two-drivers property and yields the first balance data.

REQ-WDS-30Build, serve, and deploy reuse the POC pipeline unchangedMust

The slice runs under the existing Bun + Vite + strict-TypeScript toolchain: bun run dev serves it locally, bun run build emits the self-contained dist/ with the correct Pages subpath base, and CI gates lint + core tests + build before deploying to GitHub Pages. No new pipeline; the lockfile stays committed and reproducible.

✕ Out of scope

Explicitly not in this slice: Destiny and any between-run/meta progression, additional worlds or a world-select screen, reward drafting beyond the per-Hazard rewards already defined, Progress multipliers (the x5-style payoffs), the escalation FX curve and audio, save/load, a content-authoring pipeline, and animation polish. The card-effect vocabulary is intentionally a closed enum sized to this content, not a general scripting engine; generalizing it is later design. The slice's job is to prove this loop is fun and that the core/render seam carries a real game.
