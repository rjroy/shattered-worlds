The Walker presence

REQ-VIS-111213

What

The distant figure who nears by act and steps to the foreground when drawn — pure presentation of the existing Walker card, no new rule.

  • New src/game/walker.ts — a pure proximity/foreground calculator (e.g. given actIndex and an isInHand flag, return the target transform). backdrop.ts owns the sprite and applies the transform.
  • Show the Walker sprite (from the walker texture) only when the active theme declares a walker. Position/scale from walkerProximityForAct(state.actIndex) (S4): distant silhouette in Act 1, looming by Act 3.
  • Tween on act change: compare the layer's last-seen actIndex each drawAll; when it advances, tween to the new tier (so crossing an act reads as him closing in). Suppress this tween while he is foregrounded.
  • Foreground on draw: when state.hand.some(c => c.name === 'The Walker'), switch to a large foreground presentation tied to that card. Hold keeps him there; once he leaves the hand, return to the act-tier position.
  • Resolve and discard share the same return — the mechanical difference (grants Summon Door vs forces a Door) is carried by the card, not by a distinct Walker animation.
  • All of it reads state (hand membership, actIndex) in drawAll. The state.hand membership check is sufficient for enter/exit; no event parsing, no renderer-side timer that could desync from the deck.

Validation

  • Distant silhouette in Act 1, looming by Act 3, tweening (not snapping) on the ActAdvanced boundary.
  • Foregrounds when the Walker card is forced into hand (use the dev console or sim to draw it) and returns to the backdrop after it leaves — identically whether resolved or discarded.
  • Everything is state-derived: stepping the same state through drawAll twice yields the same Walker position; there is no timer driving it.
  • walker.ts is pure (no Phaser import) and could carry a small headless unit test for the in-hand-vs-act transform if useful.

Why

The Walker is the ambient dread made literal and the visual face of the existing REQ-WDS-19/20 card: always present (REQ-VIS-11), foreground on draw (REQ-VIS-12), and strictly state-derived so the figure and the deck never disagree (REQ-VIS-13). See plan Step 9.

  • src/game/backdrop.ts edit (owns the sprite, drives it in drawAll)
