Generate theme assets & establish the asset directory

REQ-VIS-17supports 5 · 6 · 8 · 11

What

Produce the game-ready art the renderer will load and give the app its first asset directory. No code wiring in this task — assets only.

  • Copy the canon cardback.webp from .lore/assets/ into src/game/assets/cardback.webp (shared, theme-independent).
  • Use the art-gen skill on the big-box panel of .lore/assets/art-style.webp to derive three theme assets:
    • Reality backdrop — desaturated big-box interior, sized ≥ 900×600 to cover the canvas.
    • Intrusion overlay — the fluorescent-green bleed, transparent where reality should show through (this layers over the reality image; its alpha is driven later).
    • Walker silhouette — a transparent PNG (background removed) so it scales freely for the distance/foreground tween.
  • Establish the path convention src/game/assets/themes/<worldId>/… — the layout every future world follows. For this world: src/game/assets/themes/zombie-big-box/{bigbox-reality.webp, intrusion-overlay.webp, walker.png}.

Validation

  • All four files exist at the agreed paths (the shared card back plus three theme assets).
  • The Walker PNG has a real alpha channel — no opaque rectangle behind the figure.
  • Each image's dimensions cover the 900×600 canvas without upscaling blur.
  • The intrusion overlay is genuinely transparent in its non-intruded regions (open it and confirm alpha, don't assume).

Why

Every later renderer step loads these by key. The card back is the constant canon (REQ-VIS-5); the reality + intrusion layers feed the intensity-driven backdrop (REQ-VIS-8/9); the Walker silhouette is the figure (REQ-VIS-11/12). Assets resolving cleanly is REQ-VIS-17. Decision: art is generated from the concept sheet via art-gen (confirmed with user). See plan Step 1.
