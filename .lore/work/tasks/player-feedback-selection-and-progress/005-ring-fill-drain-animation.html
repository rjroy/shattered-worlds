S5

Ring fill + end-of-turn drain animation (one clock)

pending REQ-FEEDBACK-8 REQ-FEEDBACK-9

Dependencies: S4 (the ring + updateCostRing). Relies on S3's persistent container.

What

Make updateCostRing animate from the ring's currently displayed fraction to the new target instead of snapping. Because the ring persists (S3), banking and the per-turn reset are the same tween in opposite directions.

  • Track the ring's displayed fraction on the ring object. In updateCostRing, if target ≠ displayed, killTweensOf the ring then scene.tweens.add a tween over a shared duration (~200–400ms) that interpolates a { value } proxy and redraws the arc on onUpdate. First render (no prior displayed value) snaps.
  • On a progress play the post-dispatch drawAll computes a higher fraction → ring fills. On EndTurn, progress is wiped to {} (reduce.ts:129), the held hazard persists in hand (reduce.ts:127), and the post-dispatch drawAll computes fraction 0 → the same ring drains 2→0.
  • Same duration/easing both directions so they read as one clock (FEEDBACK-8); the drain must be clearly perceptible, not a 1-frame snap (FEEDBACK-9, core teaching beat).
  • No events required: the spec's FEEDBACK-9 suggested consuming TurnEnded and caching pre-reset progress because it assumed the ring is destroyed; with reuse, the ring's displayed fraction is the pre-reset value. Do not wire dispatch's returned events for this.
Drain safety invariant. A world card whose ring is mid-tween must not be destroyed/recreated, or the displayed fraction is lost and the drain snaps. S3's reconcile destroys only cards not in state.hand; a hazard still holding progress at turn-end stays in hand, so its container persists through the drain. The only world cards destroyed mid-frame are resolved hazards (HazardResolved → left the hand); those exit immediately and never drain.

Validation

  • FEEDBACK-8 (manual): bank progress on a hazard → the ring animates upward to the new fraction, not a snap.
  • FEEDBACK-9 (manual): end a turn with a hazard holding partial progress → the ring visibly drains to zero (animated), legible as "the work I banked just evaporated."
  • Fill and drain feel like the same clock (comparable duration/easing).
  • Resolve a hazard (progress reaches cost) → it exits without a separate drain beat; no orphaned ring tween.
  • bun test green; tsc/eslint clean.

Why

Implements REQ-FEEDBACK-8 (fill as banked) and REQ-FEEDBACK-9 (drain at end of turn) as one clock telling the burst-or-lose story. Per plan step S5.
