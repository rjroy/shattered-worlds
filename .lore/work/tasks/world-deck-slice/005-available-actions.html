availableActions — the one legality source

REQ-WDS-82729

What

A pure selector that answers "what is legal from here?" once, so the renderer's highlights, the reducer's validation, and the sim's policy can never disagree. availableActions(state): AvailableActions:

  • playable — for each player card in hand, derive its TargetSpec from its effect and omit cards with no legal play:
    • a DealProgress/hazard-targeting card is omitted with no Hazard in hand;
    • Panic (returnWorld 1..1) is omitted unless ≥1 world card is in hand;
    • Adrenaline (discardPlayer) is omitted with no other player card to discard;
    • Sprint stays playable (its draw branch is always available) but its hit-Slow branch is marked unavailable when no Slow Hazard exists;
    • Med Kit / Summon Door are none and always playable.
  • discardable — world cards in hand with discardable === true (excludes the Door).
  • legalTargets(cardId, step) — the concrete ids legal for a card's spec at a compound step:
    • Barricade: Hazards at step 0, remaining world cards at step 1;
    • Adrenaline: the player cards in hand other than Adrenaline itself (never world cards).
  • canEndTurn — true while status==='playing'.

The selector returns specs/ids only; it never mutates and never applies effects. It is the single definition of targeting rules.

Validation

  • A DealProgress card with no Hazard in hand is absent from playable; with a Hazard it appears with a hazard spec.
  • Panic appears only with ≥1 world card in hand; Sprint's hit branch is flagged unavailable with no Slow Hazard but the card remains playable.
  • The Door is never in discardable; other Hazards are.
  • legalTargets for Barricade returns Hazards at step 0 and remaining world cards at step 1.
  • legalTargets for Adrenaline returns the other player cards in hand — not Adrenaline itself, not any world card.

Why

The targeting design's load-bearing decision: one pure selector serves the renderer highlights, the dispatch validation (REQ-WDS-8), and the sim's legal-action policy (REQ-WDS-29), so targeting rules are never duplicated (REQ-WDS-27's shared surface). See plan Step 5 and targeting-interaction.
