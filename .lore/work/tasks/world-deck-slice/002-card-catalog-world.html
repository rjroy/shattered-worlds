Card catalog & the Zombie Big-Box world

REQ-WDS-181923242

What

Encode every card and the exact three-act world as data, plus createWorld(seed) to deal a fresh run. Pure data and deterministic setup; no turn logic.

  • Catalog as templates keyed by CardTemplateId:
    • Starter (10): Sprint ×2 (Modal[Draw 2p+1w; DealProgress 1 vs Slow]), Explore ×3 (DealProgress 1, +1 vs Hidden), Barricade ×2 (Sequence[DealProgress 1; ReturnWorldCards 0..2]), Med Kit ×1 (Heal 2), Panic ×1 (Sequence[ReturnWorldCards 1..1; Draw world 1]), Adrenaline ×1 (DiscardThenDraw 2) (REQ-WDS-23).
    • Reward/gained: Listen (DealProgress 1, +2 vs Hidden), Baseball Bat (DealProgress 2, +3 vs Creature), Regroup (DestroyCardInHand 0..1), Summon Door (AddWorldCardToTop Door); Panic/Adrenaline reused as copies (REQ-WDS-24).
    • World templates: Strange Sounds, Rubble, Screams, Zombie, Find Baseball Bat, The Walker, Door — each with cost, keywords, discardable flag, penalty, reward exactly per REQ-WDS-19.
  • mintCard(state, templateId): [Card, state'] — stamps a template with the in-state nextId and advances the counter (the deterministic id source).
  • world.ts: the Zombie Big-Box composition as three ordered acts — Act 1 (Strange Sounds×2, Rubble×2, Screams×2); Act 2 (Rubble×2, Zombie×3, Find Baseball Bat×1); Act 3 (Find Baseball Bat×1, Zombie×4, The Walker×1).
  • createWorld(seed): GameState — build the 10-card starter and shuffle (seeded) into playerDraw; mint & shuffle Act 1 into worldDraw; queue Acts 2–3; HP 20; then deal the opening hand via the Step 3 refill routine (0 held → 4 player + 2 world).

The Door is discardable:false (the only no-discard guard; penalty None), reward:SurviveWorld, cost 2. The Walker is cost 10, penalty AddWorldCardToTop(Door), reward AddPlayerCardToTop(Summon Door). The opening-deal wire-up has a forward dependency on Step 3's draw routine; compose/catalog tests below do not need it.

Validation

  • World composition is exact: the three acts match REQ-WDS-19 card-for-card — count, cost, keywords, penalty, reward.
  • Starter is the ten cards of REQ-WDS-23 with the right effects; reward templates match REQ-WDS-24.
  • Act 1 shuffle is deterministic from seed; a different seed yields a different Act 1 order.
  • Minted ids are unique across a full deal (no duplicates, REQ-WDS-2).

Why

REQ-WDS-19 (the world content is exactly this deck), REQ-WDS-23/24 (the card catalog), REQ-WDS-18 (createWorld shuffles Act 1 and queues later acts), REQ-WDS-2 (unique stable ids). This is the data the whole slice plays on. See plan Step 2.
