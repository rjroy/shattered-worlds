Draw, refill-toward-6 & act advancement

REQ-WDS-718

What

The one draw routine used by both the opening deal and every refill, plus the act-advancement rule that fires on any world draw, mid-turn or at end of turn.

  • drawPlayer(state, n) — pull from playerDraw; if it empties mid-draw, reshuffle playerDiscard (seeded) into a new pile, emit DeckShuffled, continue.
  • drawWorld(state, n) — pull from worldDraw; when empty and a world card is still required, advanceAct (shuffle the next queued act into worldDraw, bump actIndex, emit ActAdvanced{act}), continue. If no act remains and the pile is empty, draw nothing (no crash).
  • refillHand(state) — the REQ-WDS-7 table: heldWorld = hand.filter(world).length; worldToDraw = max(1, 2 - heldWorld) capped by room-toward-6 and world cards remaining; then player draws fill to 6. If skipDrawNext is set, suppress player draws this refill but still take the minimum world card and emit DrawSkipped. If the hand is already at 6 from held world cards, draw nothing. Emit CardsDrawn{ids}.
  • Wire createWorld's opening deal to call refillHand (0 held → 4+2).

Mid-turn world draws (Sprint's draw branch, Panic) reuse drawWorld, so act advancement during a turn is the same code path; a Hazard drawn mid-turn lands in hand and is targetable that turn. Held world cards are never touched by an act boundary.

Validation

  • Draw table exact: holding 0/1/2 world cards yields 4+2 / 4+1 / 3+1.
  • Skip-draw suppresses player draws but still yields the minimum one world card; a hand already at 6 draws nothing.
  • Act advances when the current act's world pile empties (emits ActAdvanced) while held world cards remain in hand; mid-turn advancement behaves identically.
  • Exhaustion is safe: driving past Act 3's last world card leaves the world pile empty and the minimum-one draw yields nothing rather than throwing.
  • Player-pile exhaustion reshuffles the player discard (seeded) and emits DeckShuffled.

Why

REQ-WDS-7 (refill toward six, always at least one world card; held-world reduction; skip-draw) and REQ-WDS-18 (act advancement empties one act into the next, applies to any world draw, leaves held cards alone, exhausts without crashing). A single draw path prevents the opening-deal/turn-draw divergence bug. See plan Step 3.
